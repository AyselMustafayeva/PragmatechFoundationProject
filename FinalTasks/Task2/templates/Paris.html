Paris
In the 18th century, the cemeteries of the ever-growing city of Paris lacked space. Some of the bodies were not properly buried and spread the disease. Ultimately, Parisian officials decided to ban city cemeteries and move the remains that were on them to another location.
Officials drew attention to several underground quarries in the city. Between the 1780s and 1814, the authorities were able to organize the underground transport of more than six million bodies collected from all cemeteries in Paris, transporting the dead with carts and placing them in their final resting place.
