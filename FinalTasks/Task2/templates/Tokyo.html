Tokyo
The water in the street canals in Japan is very clean, no garbage is dumped into it, and no sewage gets into it. In terms of quality, it is even better than tap water, and fish swim in it. The groundwater that flows into these channels can be used to cool drinks and prepare food. This system is called kabata. In Shiga prefecture, the channels have different heights - in the lower part of them they wash dishes, and fish feed on the remains of food. And if you leave a dirty plate for 3 hours, then you won't have to wash it: the carps will eat everything without a trace.
Such channels are also a landmark in Japan.
